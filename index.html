About the Founder

Shaima Hussein Mustafa, a psychologist and a mother to a child with autism, founded Bright Future Organization with the aim to uplift women and families with disabilities in Duhok. Her personal journey drives the mission to create safe spaces, offer empowerment, and build opportunities for those often left behind.

Our Mission

We aim to empower women and individuals with disabilities by providing them the tools and support needed to live dignified, independent lives. Through vocational training, psychosocial support, and human rights awareness, we are committed to a future of inclusion and opportunity.

Our Long-Term Goal

We strive to eliminate the barriers disabled individuals face, and to build a society where women are uplifted, self-reliant, and confident in their abilities.

Our Project: Empowering Women in Duhok

Status: Proposal Submitted (Awaiting Approval)

This 6-month project aims to support 60 vulnerable women—especially those with disabilities and mothers of disabled children—through hands-on skills training, psychological support, and self-advocacy workshops.

  • Vocational training (tailoring, handicrafts, food production, phone repair)
  • Workshops on human rights and women's empowerment
  • Psychosocial support groups and therapy sessions
  • Final exhibition showcasing participants' products

The project is expected to directly benefit over 60 women and indirectly impact over 700 individuals within their families and communities.
